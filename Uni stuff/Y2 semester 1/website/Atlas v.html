Atlas v

The Atlas v is an expendable rocket and is a member of the large atlas family of rockets operated by the joint Boeing and Lockheed-martin joint venture United Launch Alliance (ULA), it has many different configurations and can lift a large variety of cargo.

one of the most interesting details of the Atlas v is the many configurations it can launch in, each of these can have different numbers of boosters, any number from 0 to 5 and the number of second stage engines, this allows it to lift large payloads, or even send cargo to Mars whilst also not being wasteful when launching smaller payloads.

this flexibility in configuration is reflected in the variety of cargo that can be placed into orbit, with past launches carrying the new horizons probe, weather satellites, comsats, signals intelligence satellites, the Mars reconnaissance orbiter, research satellites, spy satellites, lunar explorer probes, the juno jupiter orbiter, the curiosity rover, the x-37b orbital spyplane and iss cargo ships, among many others, it is also planned to launch humans in the future using Boeing's CTS-100 capsule.
